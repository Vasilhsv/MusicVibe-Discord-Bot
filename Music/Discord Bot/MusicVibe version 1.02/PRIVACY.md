# Privacy Policy for MusicVibe

**Last Updated: March 02, 2026**

### 1. Introduction
This Privacy Policy describes how **MusicVibe** ("the Bot") collects, stores, and protects your data in accordance with the **EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** and Discord's Developer Policy.

### 2. Data We Collect & Store
To provide the personalized leveling and music experience, we store the following information in a private, secure SQLite database:
* **User Identity**: Your unique Discord User ID and Username.
* **Personal Statistics**: Total commands used, total hours spent in voice channels, current Level, and total Experience Points (XP).
* **Server Information**: The date and time you joined the server (Join Date).

### 3. Data Privacy & Access
* **Private Access**: Your statistics are for your eyes only. They are accessible exclusively to you via our web dashboard after a successful Discord Login.
* **No Public Exposure**: We do not share your individual progress, levels, or voice activity with other users or third parties.
* **No Content Logging**: We do not record voice audio or log private chat messages.

### 4. Purpose of Processing
We process your data solely to:
* **Maintain Your Profile**: To track your XP and Level progression over time.
* **Web Integration**: To allow you to view your personal stats on the MusicVibe dashboard.
* **Security**: To identify and prevent bot abuse or spamming.

### 5. Your Rights & Data Erasure (GDPR)
Under GDPR, you have the "Right to be Forgotten":
* **Manual Deletion**: You can permanently and instantly delete all your data (XP, Level, Stats) from our database by using the **`/forgetme`** command.
* **Data Access**: You can view all information we store about you via the **`/rank`** command or by logging into the website.

### 6. Security & Storage
All user data is stored on a **private, dedicated server** located in the EU (Greece). We implement industry-standard measures to ensure the `musicvibe.sqlite` database remains secure.

### 7. Third-Party Services
The Bot uses **yt-dlp** to stream music from YouTube/SoundCloud. No personal identifiable information is shared with these platforms during the streaming process.
